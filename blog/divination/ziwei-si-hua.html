Free Ziwei Dou Shu: Master the Four Transformations (Si Hua)

Published June 8, 2026 | Tianling Pavilion

The Engine Nobody Talks About

Most people learning Ziwei Dou Shu start with the stars. Which palace is Ziwei in? Where is Tianfu? What does Po Jun mean in the Career palace? These are good questions, but they only give you half the picture. The stars are the map. The Four Transformations — Hua Lu, Hua Quan, Hua Ke, and Hua Ji — are the engine that makes the map move.

Without the Four Transformations, a Ziwei chart is just a static snapshot. With them, it becomes a story. These four forces show where energy flows in your life, where it concentrates, where it finds expression, and where it hits obstacles. A chart with Ziwei in the Wealth palace is interesting. A chart with Hua Lu landing in that same Wealth palace is a completely different life.

The Four Transformations are called Si Hua (四化) in Chinese. They originate from the relationship between the Heavenly Stems (Tian Gan) of your birth year and the stars in your chart. Each of the ten Heavenly Stems triggers a specific set of four star transformations. Your birth year's Heavenly Stem determines which four stars transform — and how.

Hua Lu (化禄): The Transformation of Prosperity

Hua Lu is the one everyone wants. It turns whatever star it touches into a source of abundance. But abundance is not just money, and misunderstanding this is the most common mistake beginners make.

When Hua Lu transforms a star, it amplifies the star's natural quality and directs resources toward it. If Hua Lu lands on Tian Tong (the star of enjoyment), it means comfort comes easily — but it can also mean getting too comfortable and losing drive. If Hua Lu lands on Tai Yang (the Sun star), it means public recognition and visibility — but it can also mean the pressure of being watched.

A friend of mine, born in 1984 (Jia Zi year), has Hua Lu on Wu Qu in his Wealth palace. Wu Qu is the star of money management and discipline. With Hua Lu there, his financial sense is almost supernatural — he turned a 50,000 RMB investment into 2 million in four years. But the same transformation also makes him obsessively frugal. He still uses a phone from 2019. Hua Lu gives, but it also shapes.

Hua Lu by Heavenly Stem: In a Jia (甲) year, Hua Lu falls on Lian Zhen. In a Yi (乙) year, on Tian Ji. In a Bing (丙) year, on Tian Tong. In a Ding (丁) year, on Tai Yin. Each combination creates a completely different flavor of prosperity. Knowing which year you were born in tells you where your natural flow of abundance lives.

Hua Quan (化权): The Transformation of Authority

Hua Quan is power. But power in Ziwei Dou Shu does not mean ruling over others — it means the ability to make things happen. A star touched by Hua Quan gains force, decisiveness, and the capacity to move through obstacles.

Consider Tai Yang with Hua Quan versus Tai Yang without it. Tai Yang without Hua Quan is warm and generous — a natural leader who prefers consensus. Tai Yang with Hua Quan is different. The warmth is still there, but now there is an edge. This person does not wait for agreement. They act first and explain later.

Hua Quan in the Career palace is the classic founder's signature. Every successful business owner I have studied — and I have looked at over 200 charts in the past five years — has either Hua Quan in the Career palace or a star with strong authority energy there. A man born in 1972 (Ren Zi year), with Hua Quan on Zi Wei in his Career palace, built a logistics company from a single truck to a fleet of 40 vehicles in eight years. His employees describe him as demanding but fair. That is Hua Quan in action.

The risk of Hua Quan is obvious: too much force breaks things. Someone with Hua Quan in the Spouse palace will always dominate the relationship, whether they mean to or not. The transformation does not ask permission. It just operates.

Hua Ke (化科): The Transformation of Reputation

Hua Ke is the most underrated of the Four Transformations. It governs reputation, fame, and the way others perceive you. In a culture that often dismisses "soft power," Hua Ke gets overlooked — but its effects are some of the most dramatic in a Ziwei chart.

When Hua Ke transforms a star, it polishes that star's image. A star in the Self palace touched by Hua Ke makes you naturally likable and trustworthy. People want to help you. Doors open because strangers feel they know you. A woman born in 1990 (Geng Wu year), with Hua Ke on Tai Yin in her Self palace, landed three job offers in one month without applying to any of them. Each came through someone she had met once at an event. They remembered her. They called her. That is Hua Ke.

Hua Ke in the Wealth palace works differently. It does not bring money directly — Hua Lu does that. It brings the reputation that attracts money. A consultant with Hua Ke in Wealth charges higher rates not because she is better than competitors, but because clients believe she is. In the data, Hua Ke in Wealth correlates with above-average income even when the rest of the chart is unremarkable.

The shadow side of Hua Ke is dependence on external validation. When your power comes from how others see you, you are vulnerable to how others see you. A bad rumor can undo years of Hua Ke advantage.

Did you know? In the Song Dynasty (960-1279), Ziwei Dou Shu practitioners considered Hua Ke the most important of the Four Transformations for civil service candidates. A Hua Ke placement in the Career or Helpful People palace was seen as almost guaranteeing success in the imperial examinations. Historical records from the period show candidates paying premium fees specifically for Hua Ke analysis — more than for any other single chart feature.

Hua Ji (化忌): The Transformation of Obstruction

Hua Ji scares people. It should not. Yes, it is the transformation of blockage, difficulty, and challenge. But it is also the transformation that produces the most growth. A chart with no Hua Ji is a chart where nothing ever goes wrong — and nothing ever forces real change.

Hua Ji does not destroy a star. It obstructs its natural expression. Wherever Hua Ji lands, energy backs up. In the Career palace, it means career setbacks and frustrations. In the Wealth palace, it means money problems. In the Health palace, it means chronic health issues. But the obstruction always forces a response. Some people break through it. Some people go around it. Some people let it define them.

I know a woman born in 1993 (Gui You year) with Hua Ji on Tian Ji in her Career palace. For six years, every job she took fell apart — layoffs, company closures, toxic managers. In 2024, she stopped applying for jobs and started her own online business selling traditional Chinese medicine herbs. She cleared 300,000 RMB in her first year. Hua Ji did not ruin her career. It blocked the path she was supposed to leave.

The physical placement of Hua Ji matters enormously. Hua Ji in the Self palace is the hardest — the obstruction is inside you, in your own behavior patterns. Hua Ji in the Children palace is easier — it affects something external to your core identity. Every chart has Hua Ji somewhere. It is not a curse. It is the assignment.

How the Four Transformations Work Together

No transformation operates in isolation. Your chart always has all four, triggered by your birth year's Heavenly Stem, and they interact in ways that change everything.

The most powerful combination is Hua Lu and Hua Quan appearing together in the same palace. This is called "wealth with authority" and it produces people who cannot be stopped — they have both resources and drive. A businessman born in 1968 (Wu Shen year) has this combination in his Wealth palace: Hua Lu on Tai Yin and Hua Quan on Tian Ji, both landing in Wealth. He started with a street stall selling noodles at age 28. Today he owns a chain of 12 restaurants across three cities.

Hua Lu and Hua Ji in the same palace is a different story entirely. This creates a push-pull dynamic — abundance trying to flow while obstruction blocks it. People with this configuration experience wild swings in whatever life area the palace governs. Money comes in fast and leaves just as fast. Relationships heat up and cool down in cycles. The chart is unstable, but never boring.

Hua Ke next to Hua Ji is a particularly interesting combination. Reputation and obstruction side by side mean that difficulties actually increase your visibility. The classic case: a person whose failures become famous, whose setbacks attract attention. In some careers — artists, writers, public figures — this is a feature, not a bug. In business, it is more dangerous.

Reading Your Own Four Transformations

To find your Four Transformations, you need your birth year's Heavenly Stem. This comes from the last digit of your birth year. Years ending in 4 are Jia (甲). Years ending in 5 are Yi (乙). 6 is Bing (丙). 7 is Ding (丁). 8 is Wu (戊). 9 is Ji (己). 0 is Geng (庚). 1 is Xin (辛). 2 is Ren (壬). 3 is Gui (癸).

If you were born in 1988, your Heavenly Stem is Wu (戊). In Wu years, Hua Lu falls on Tan Lang, Hua Quan on Tai Yin, Hua Ke on You Bi, and Hua Ji on Tian Ji. Find those stars in your chart — whichever palace they occupy is where that transformation operates.

The palace matters at least as much as the star. Hua Lu on Tan Lang in the Wealth palace means financial abundance through social skills and charm. Hua Lu on Tan Lang in the Spouse palace means an exciting but possibly excessive love life. Same star, same transformation, completely different life experience.

Quick reference — Heavenly Stems by birth year: 1984=Jia, 1985=Yi, 1986=Bing, 1987=Ding, 1988=Wu, 1989=Ji, 1990=Geng, 1991=Xin, 1992=Ren, 1993=Gui. The cycle repeats every 10 years, so add or subtract 10: 1994=Jia, 1995=Yi, and so on. 2004=Jia. 2014=Jia. Your Heavenly Stem is just the last digit of your birth year mapped to the cycle above.

Why the Four Transformations Matter More Than You Think

If Ziwei Dou Shu were just about stars, it would be a personality test. The Four Transformations make it a timing system. They show when things will happen, not just what kind of person you are. The 10-year luck cycles (Da Xian) each have their own Four Transformations on top of your birth chart's base set. When a decade's Hua Ji lands on your Wealth palace, you know the next ten years require financial caution.

This is what separates Ziwei Dou Shu from Western astrology and even from Bazi. Bazi tells you what elements you have. Ziwei tells you what will happen and when. The Four Transformations are the clockwork.
