Here's what the Duke of Zhou actually teaches: the STATE of the water is everything. Dreaming of a clear, flowing stream? Things are moving. 👃Today's Focus: The Nose — The Palace of Wealth

Chen Tuan says: on a Monday, you check the nose. Why? Because the nose is the Palace of Wealth (财帛宫, Cáibó Gōng) in Mian Xiang — and Monday sets the financial tone for your whole week. Here's what most people don't know: the nose doesn't just predict wealth. It reveals how you RECEIVE. A straight, well-proportioned nose bridge (山根, shāngēn) that doesn't dip at the root signals someone who takes in good fortune without guilt. A fleshy, rounded nose tip (准头, zhǔntóu) — what the old masters call the "hanging gall bladder" shape — marks a person who accumulates rather than leaks. But a sharp, bony nose tip? You earn well but spend faster. The nostrils are the real secret: if they're visible from the front, you're generous to a fault — check your bank account. If they're hidden, you hold onto money naturally. Nostrils too large and flared? Risk-taker with money. Too small and pinched? Overly cautious with it. Self-check: look in a mirror. Are your nostrils even? Uneven nostrils suggest an uneven relationship with money — feast and famine cycles. The fix isn't surgery. It's awareness. When you know you tend to leak, you plug the holes.
