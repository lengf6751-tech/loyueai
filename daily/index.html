Ge Xuan here. Yesterday we talked about the sun. Today we're talking about a candle. Together, they make your Fire sustainable rather than explosive.

Now by Day Master. Wood Day Masters (甲, 乙) — today, your Wood feeds the day's Fire. But unlike yesterday when Fire was overwhelming, today's Fire is gentle. You give without being drained. 乙卯 (Yin Wood Rabbit) Day Masters — this is YOUR branch day (卯). Your energy is amplified. Trust your instincts. Fire Day Masters (丙, 丁) — 丁火 Day Masters, today is YOUR element day. Like finding a familiar restaurant in a foreign city. 丙火 Day Masters, yesterday's intensity fades into today's warmth. You can breathe. Earth Day Masters (戊, 己) — Fire generates Earth. Today's Yin Fire generates your Earth without overwhelming it. Steady growth, not forced expansion. Metal Day Masters (庚, 辛) — Fire melts Metal, but Yin Fire is gentler than Yang Fire. Metal isn't destroyed today — it's softened. Like a blacksmith heating metal to shape it, not melt it. You're being shaped into something more useful. Let the heat work. Water Day Masters (壬, 癸) — Water controls Fire, but today's Fire is small. Your Water energy is the boss, but don't be a bully. A candle flame doesn't need to be drowned — a gentle breath is enough. Be the breath today, not the flood.

🧭 Qimen — Today's Strategic Directions

Sa Shoujian deploys the Qimen matrix

🚪Auspicious Door: East — Sheng Men (Growth Door)

The Door of Growth (Sheng Men, 生门) opens in the east today. And I love this door for a 丁卯 day. Sheng Men is the door of new beginnings, fresh starts, and living energy — it's associated with the Wood element, which perfectly complements today's Rabbit (卯) Wood. Yesterday we used Xiu Men (Rest), which was right for a Fire-heavy day. Today, a 丁卯 day with balanced Wood-Fire energy, Sheng Men says: start something. Not something big — something small. A new habit. A new conversation. A new direction in a project that's been stuck. The Tian Fu Star (天辅星, Star of Assistance) accompanies Sheng Men in the east today. Tian Fu is one of the most benevolent stars in the Qimen system — it represents help from teachers, mentors, and guides. On a Rabbit day, this is amplified. The Rabbit is the sign of diplomacy and learning. Today, if you ask for help, you'll get it. If you offer help, it will be received. Practical advice: make one call or send one message to someone who has more experience than you in something you're working on. Ask a specific question. The east door plus the Assistance star means: the answer is waiting, but you have to reach for it. Face east when making that call or writing that message.

⛔Door to Avoid: North — Du Men (Blockade Door)

The Door of Blockade (Du Men, 杜门) opens in the north today. Du Men is the door of obstacles, dead ends, and barriers. Here's the interesting thing about Du Men: it's not trying to hurt you. It's like a locked door that's locked for a reason — behind it is something you're not ready for yet. On a 丁卯 day, when the energy is about looking closely at what's in front of you, Du Men in the north says: don't look for shortcuts. The northern path today is blocked not because it's impossible, but because going that way would teach you the wrong lesson. The Tian Xin Star (天心星, Star of the Heart/Mind) accompanies Du Men. Tian Xin is the star of strategy and medicine — it represents the heart of the matter. Together, Du Men and Tian Xin say: the obstacle IS the lesson. You don't go around it. You study it. Practical advice: if something frustrates you today — a website that won't load, a form you can't submit, a door that won't open — don't force it. Step back and ask: what is this obstacle trying to tell me? The answer is usually simpler than you think. Also avoid the northeast (Kai Men is contested there). If you need to travel, go east or south. Not north.

A problem reveals its solution. Keep a notebook by your bed tonight. And avoid the Wu Hour (午时, 11 AM–1 PM) — the Horse hour. 卯午破 (Rabbit-Horse break) makes this the most confusing hour of the day. Don't schedule anything important between 11 and 1. Take a long lunch instead.

🌙 Zhou Gong — Dream Symbol of the Day

The Duke of Zhou interprets the symbol the cosmos sends tonight

🕯️Today's Symbol: A Candle — The Light That Doesn't Shout

The Duke of Zhou says dreaming of a candle on a 丁卯 day (Yin Fire Rabbit) is one of the most elegant signs the cosmos can send. A candle is Yin Fire — contained, purposeful, intimate. Unlike a torch or a bonfire, a candle doesn't impose. It offers. And on a Rabbit day, which is about sensitivity and attention to detail, the candle says: look closer at whatever you saw in your dream. Here's what different candle dreams mean. A candle burning steadily — your inner light is stable. You are in a period of quiet, sustainable power. Not dramatic, but reliable. A candle flickering — uncertainty is affecting your emotional state. Something or someone is making you doubt your inner knowing. Don't. The flicker is the wind outside, not the flame inside. A candle going out — a chapter is ending. Not a death — a transition. The candle that burns out has fulfilled its purpose. Light the next one without mourning the old. A candle in a lantern — your light is protected. You may feel restricted, but the restriction is keeping your flame alive in conditions that would extinguish an open candle. Trust the container. A candle with a very long wick — you're burning more than you need to. Trim the excess. Say no to one thing today to protect your energy for what matters. A candle melting into a pool of wax — you have been generous to the point of depletion. The wax is the residue of your giving. On a Rabbit day, the Duke says: rest is not retreat. The pool of wax is a bed for the next candle. A candle floating on water — spirit and emotion are balanced. Fire on Water is an advanced alchemical image — it means you can feel deeply without being consumed by feeling. This is rare and precious. A room full of candles — you are surrounded by small, genuine sources of warmth. Not one big source — many small ones. Your community is your wealth today. A candle in a storm — something external is testing your inner peace. The storm won't last, and the candle is still burning. A single match lighting a candle — the moment of ignition. A new idea, a new connection, a new direction is about to be sparked. Today, on a Rabbit day, that spark will take root quietly and grow. Don't announce it. Nurture it. And Zhou Gong adds one more wisdom: if you dream of a candle today, before you fall asleep tonight, light a real candle (or even a tea light) and let it burn for three minutes while you sit in silence. Watch the flame. The Rabbit energy responds to ritual, not to effort. The candle sees what you can't. Trust it.

A long, clear Head Line reaching across the entire palm — you think things through completely. On a Yin Fire day, your thought process is especially thorough. Don't rush your decisions — your thoroughness is your superpower today. A short Head Line (ending under the Apollo finger) — you trust your gut and decide quickly. Today, combine that instinct with a moment of pause. The Rabbit says: look once, look twice, then act. Your instinct is right — but the second look gives you the detail you might have missed. A curved/sloping Head Line — creative, intuitive, artistic. You think in images, not words. Today, your visual intelligence is heightened. Draw something. Start nothing you haven't thought through. The Head Line separated from the Life Line at its start — you're independent and adventurous in your thinking. Today, your independence is your greatest asset. You see possibilities that cautious minds miss. And Xu Fu's final observation: look at the color of your Head Line today. If it's pinkish or slightly flushed — your Yin Fire is active and healthy. If it's pale — rest your mind. The candle has burned low. Read a book. Take a nap. The wisdom will return tomorrow.

☯️ I Ching — Hexagram of the Day

King Wen of Zhou casts the ancient oracle

🪙Hexagram #35 — 晋 / Jìn — Progress (Fire Over Earth)

䷢ 晋 / Jìn — Progress (The Sun Rises Over the Earth). King Wen speaks: "The enlightened prince promotes virtue and rewards merit. The fire on the earth shines far." You know why this hexagram is perfect for today? Yesterday we drew Hexagram #5 — Waiting. Waiting on the ground. Waiting with patience. Today, the waiting ends. 晋 (Jìn) means Progress — advancing, growing, rising. The image is 火地晋 — Fire over Earth. The sun (Fire) rising above the great plain (Earth). Everything becomes visible. The shadows recede. And on a 丁卯 day — Yin Fire Rabbit — this hexagram's message is beautifully specific. The Fire is not the sun (yesterday's Yang Fire). The Fire here is more like the light of dawn — the moment when the sky is no longer dark but the sun hasn't fully broken the horizon. That in-between light. The Rabbit's favorite time. The light by which you can see clearly but not be fully seen. Today, the hexagram says: you don't need to be at your destination. You just need to be MOVING toward it. Progress, not arrival.

King Wen's judgment: "The powerful prince is honored with horses in great numbers. In a single day he is received three times." This is not a metaphor for literal horses. In the I Ching, horses represent strength, nobility, and the capacity to carry heavy things. Today, you will receive the strength you need — not all at once, but in installments. Three times. Three specific moments when the universe shows up for you. You will also receive recognition — the "three audiences" with the prince mean that your efforts will be seen and acknowledged by people in authority. Not because you asked for it. Because the light of dawn reveals everything that's already there. Line 1 of this hexagram says: "Progressing, but turned back. Perseverance brings good fortune." You may try to move forward today and feel like you're being pulled back. That's not failure. That's the hexagram's first line testing your resolve. The pullback is like a bowstring being drawn — the arrow hasn't moved forward yet, but tension is building. Stay sincere. Stay persistent. The release is coming. Line 4 says: "Progressing like a hamster. Perseverance brings danger." This is the warning line. A hamster hoards — it takes and takes and never gives. On a Rabbit day, when the energy is about receiving, Line 4 warns: don't be greedy. Don't take more than you need. A hamster's hoarding looks like preparation but it's actually fear. Today, receive what comes, share what you have, and trust that the supply is infinite. Line 6 says: "Progressing with the horns. But you are allowed to attack your own city." The horns are the most advanced position — far ahead, exposed, isolated. If you find yourself at the front of the pack today, uncertain and alone: the hexagram says you're allowed to turn around. You're allowed to retreat to safety. Progress doesn't always mean forward motion. Sometimes progress is knowing when to stop charging so you can see where you're actually going. The old masters said: on a 丁卯 day, progress is measured not by distance traveled, but by clarity gained. Go one mile with clear eyes rather than ten miles in a fog. That one mile contains everything you need to find.
