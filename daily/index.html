😶 Face Reading — Today's Feature

Chen Tuan reads the map of your face

🤨Today's Focus: The Eyebrows — The Palace of Siblings and Authority

Chen Tuan says: on a Metal day, you check the brows. Here's why — eyebrows are associated with Metal in Mian Xiang (面相). They represent authority, longevity, and how you relate to siblings and peers. Long, flowing brows that extend past the outer corners of the eyes? That's the "silkworm eyebrow" — an omen of steady authority that grows with age. Short, abrupt brows that don't reach the eye corner? You make quick decisions and quick enemies. Balanced brows — about a finger's width apart — signal someone who trusts others at the right pace. Brows too close together (less than the width of one finger)? You worry. A lot. About things that usually don't happen. Brows too far apart? You trust too easily and get burned. The old masters also looked at brow texture. Smooth, glossy brows = smooth interactions today. Bristly or disordered brows = expect friction in communication. Self-check right now: run your finger across your eyebrow. If it feels rough or unruly, soften your approach to people today. If it's smooth, speak your mind without apology. The brows never lie — they're the first thing the gaze meets, and Chen Tuan says they're the most honest feature on your face.
